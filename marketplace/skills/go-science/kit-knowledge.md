# KIT System — Complete Knowledge Base

## What KIT Is

KIT = Keep In Touch. It is the 50–60 annual touchpoints system that keeps you top-of-mind with your entire database — TYO (Traffic You Own). KIT is NOT marketing. It is NOT selling. It is relationship maintenance that creates the conditions for sales to happen naturally.

**The core principle:** People buy from people they remember and trust. KIT makes you the person they remember when the need arises — so when they are ready, they call you first.

---

## The Three Rules of KIT

1. **No pitch. No pressure. No ask.** Every message that feels like marketing destroys 3 previous messages.
2. **It is for them, not for you.** The message must give something — a thought, a feeling, a memory, a smile — before it can ask for anything.
3. **Series, not blast.** One message does nothing. 50–60 touches a year creates a relationship. Frequency without quality is spam. Quality without frequency is forgotten.

---

## The 5 Client Segments for KIT

Each segment gets a different tone, intent, and message type.

| Segment | Who They Are | KIT Goal | Tone |
|---------|-------------|---------|------|
| **Active Client** | Currently paying, project ongoing | Strengthen relationship, increase referrals, reduce churn | Warm, peer-to-peer, value-add |
| **Past Client (Amicable)** | Project ended well, positive relationship | Reactivate interest, stay relevant, referral source | Nostalgic + forward-looking, zero pressure |
| **Past Client (Hold/Pending)** | Project paused, may resume | Keep warm, wait for right moment, no pressure | Patient, empathetic, genuinely caring |
| **Past Client (Problematic)** | Bad ending, TSM fault or client fault | Acknowledge, rebuild trust from zero, very slow | Accountable, humble, no business talk |
| **Lead (Not Yet Client)** | Enquired but never bought | Nurture curiosity, stay in their world | Educational + interesting, no pitch |

## The 4-Part Invisible Message Structure

Every good KIT message has this structure — the reader never sees it, but it is always there:

```
HOOK → BRIDGE → COMPLIMENT → SOFT OPEN DOOR
```

**Hook:** Something real in the world — an event, a trend, a festival, a memory, a news item, a thought — that creates a reason to be in touch. The hook is never "I was thinking of you." It is specific and external.

**Bridge:** A natural connection from the hook to the person/brand. "That made me think of [Name/Brand]." The bridge must feel obvious and genuine, not manufactured.

**Compliment:** One specific, genuine thing you appreciate about what they do — framed around the value they create for others. Never flattery. Always specific. 

**Soft Open Door:** A closing that creates zero pressure but leaves a door open. "No agenda. Just reconnecting." OR "If you're ever open to a conversation, I'm here." OR simply "Hope all is well on your side."

---

## The Hook Types — 12 Angles

These are the 12 angles to approach a KIT message from. Rotate across them so no two consecutive messages feel the same.

| # | Hook Type | What It Is | When to Use |
|---|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | **Industry Trend** | Something changing in their industry right now | Best for B2B clients, manufacturers, service businesses |
| 2 | **Festival/Date** | A culturally relevant celebration or national day | Match to religion/region of the client |
| 3 | **Personal Date** | Birthday, work anniversary, project anniversary | Most impactful — extremely personal |
| 4 | **News/Event** | A recent news event that touches their world | Must be genuinely relevant — never forced |
| 5 | **Memory Recall** | Something specific you remember about them/their brand | Works beautifully for long-lost contacts |
| 6 | **Random Thought** | A genuine observation that reminded you of them | Most authentic feeling when done right |
| 7 | **Education/Insight** | A useful piece of information for their world | Builds Reality (R) in ARC — positions expertise |
| 8 | **Story Share** | A quick story (yours or someone else's) relevant to them | Builds Affinity (A) — most emotional hook |
| 9 | **Resource/Book/Tool** | Sharing something useful — a book, article, tool | Highest value-add type |
| 10 | **Physical Touch** | A letter, a book, a small gift sent physically | Rarest, highest impact — use once a year maximum |
| 11 | **Social Proof Mention** | "I was sharing your work with someone recently..." | Builds status for them — extremely flattering |
| 12 | **Accountability** | Owning something that went wrong in the past | Use ONLY for problematic endings — powerful reset |

## The Psychographic-to-Hook Mapping

Use the client's psychographic profile to choose the hook angle:

| Psychographic Trait | Best Hook Angles |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Makes decisions on price | Industry Trend (cost/value angle), Education/Insight |
| Values luxury | Social Proof Mention, Physical Touch, Memory Recall |
| Positive attitude | Random Thought, Story Share, Festival |
| Negative attitude | Education/Insight, Industry Trend (validation angle) |
| Health conscious | Random Thought (health angle), Resource/Book/Tool |
| Loves cars | Random Thought (automotive/precision angle) |
| Travels a lot | Festival (regional), News/Event (travel/global) |

## The KIT Sequence — What Goes at Each Touch

Every sequence starts when the first KIT is sent. The 15-day cadence is the target.

| KIT Number | Tone | Recommended Hook Type | Length |
|-----------|------|----------------------|--------|
| KIT 1 | Warmest — pure reconnect | Memory Recall OR Festival/Date OR Random Thought | Short (5–8 lines) |
| KIT 2 | Follow the energy of KIT 1 response | Industry Trend OR Education/Insight | Medium (8–12 lines) |
| KIT 3 | Slightly more personal | Story Share OR Random Thought | Short–medium |
| KIT 4 | If no response — reset energy. If response — deeper | Resource/Book/Tool OR Physical Touch | Medium |
| KIT 5 | If no response — one final warm touch. If response — move to next stage | Accountability (if needed) OR Personal Date | Short |

**After 5 touches with zero response:** Pause KIT, move to "dormant" status. Revisit in 3 months with a single neutral message.

## The Response Logic

What happens AFTER the client responds changes everything.

| Response Type | What It Means | Next Action |
|--------------|--------------|------------|
| **Warm reply** ("Great to hear from you!") | High engagement — they remembered you positively | Respond naturally, continue conversation, next KIT can be warmer/more direct |
| **Short acknowledgment** ("Thanks!") | Polite but not re-engaged | Next KIT: try a different hook type, more personal angle |
| **Question or curiosity** ("What are you up to these days?") | Signal of interest — potential re-engagement | Respond conversationally, introduce current work naturally, no pitch |
| **No response** | Not yet — keep going | Change hook type next time, don't repeat similar angle |
| **"Please stop messaging"** | Clear signal | Mark as STOP KIT immediately |
| **Business-related reply** ("Actually, I was thinking about...") | Sales signal | Move to sales conversation mode — hand off to team |

## The Channel Selection Logic

| Channel | Use When | Avoid When |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **WhatsApp** | Primary channel for Indian contacts, warm relationship | International contacts who don't use WhatsApp |
| **Email** | Formal relationships, international clients, corporate contacts | Someone who preferred WhatsApp in the original project |
| **Phone Call** | Active clients, high-value reconnects after 3+ messages | Cold contacts, lost clients on first touch |
| **Physical (Letter/Book)** | Once a year for high-value lost clients, A-grade clients | Everyone — this must be rare to have impact |

## The STOP KIT Rules

Immediately mark a client as STOP KIT if:
1. Client explicitly says "don't contact me"
2. Client has an outstanding payment dispute that is legally active
3. Client is currently in a legal dispute with TSM
4. Client gave an extremely hostile or abusive response

Mark as PAUSE KIT (review before sending) if:
1. Client recently had a major personal loss
2. Client's business closed or went bankrupt
3. Client is in a known health crisis
4. KIT 5 is complete with zero response — wait 90 days before resuming

## Festival Calendar Framework

Festival messages follow the same structure but with one additional rule: **The festival fact must be interesting, non-obvious, and relevant to their world.** A generic "Wishing you a Happy Diwali" is NOT KIT. A message that starts with an interesting historical fact about Diwali and bridges it to their industry IS KIT.

**Religion-to-Festival Mapping:**
- **Hindu:** Diwali, Holi, Dussehra, Makar Sankranti, Ram Navami, Navratri, Ganesh Chaturthi, Janmashtami
- **Muslim:** Eid ul-Fitr, Eid ul-Adha, Ramadan start
- **Christian:** Christmas, Easter, Good Friday
- **Sikh:** Baisakhi, Guru Nanak Jayanti, Hola Mohalla
- **Jain:** Paryushana, Mahavir Jayanti
- **All religions:** Republic Day (26 Jan), Independence Day (15 Aug), Gandhi Jayanti (2 Oct), New Year (1 Jan)

**Do NOT send a Hindu festival message to a Muslim client.** Cross-reference the religion column.

## The Image Attachment Rules

Images amplify a message when used correctly. They destroy it when used incorrectly.

**Use an image when:**
- It is a festival message and a clean, branded graphic exists
- A visual reference makes the hook stronger (e.g., a news screenshot, a book cover)
- It is a physical KIT and the image is a photo of the item being sent

**Never use an image when:**
- You are sending a personal reconnect message (images make it feel like a campaign)
- The image is generic stock photography
- The message is about accountability or sensitivity

## Data Required Per Client for Effective KIT

**Minimum required (KIT will work but limited):**
- Name + addressed_as
- Company name + what they actually do
- Client segment (active/lost-amicable/lost-hold/lost-problematic/lead)
- Preferred communication channel
- Accountable person (who sends the message)
- Last contact date

**Recommended (KIT becomes significantly more personalized):**
- Psychographic traits
- Religion
- DOB (birthday)
- DOA (project start/anniversary date)
- Leaving month (if lost client)
- Brief description of how relationship ended (for lost clients)
- Industry context / what's happening in their industry

**Full (KIT becomes highly personalized and responsive):**
- Complete KIT history (what was sent, when, hook type used)
- Response log (what they said, sentiment)
- Social media profiles (for research on what they're up to)
- Google Doc link (project chronology)
- Any specific memories or inside references

## KIT Message Quality Checklist

Before sending any KIT message, verify:
- [ ] No pitch, no offer, no price mentioned
- [ ] No pressure language ("I'd love to work with you again", "Let me know if you need anything")
- [ ] Hook is specific and real — not generic
- [ ] Bridge to the person/brand feels natural, not manufactured
- [ ] Compliment is specific to what they actually do
- [ ] Closing is warm but pressure-free
- [ ] Language matches preferred language of client
- [ ] Correct name/salutation used (addressed_as column)
- [ ] Signed by the correct accountable person
- [ ] Not repeating the same hook type as last message
- [ ] Festival messages cross-checked against client's religion
